FINNISH TAX DEDUCTION RULES AND REGULATIONS - 2024
1. HOME OFFICE DEDUCTIONS (Työhuonevähennys)
==============================================
Self-employed individuals and entrepreneurs working from home may claim a deduction
for a portion of housing costs (rent or mortgage interest) and utilities.
Requirements:
- The home office must be a dedicated space used regularly for work
- At least 50% of the room must be used for business purposes
- The deduction is typically calculated per square meter used
Deductible expenses:
- Rent (for rented space)
- Mortgage interest (not principal)
- Utilities: electricity, heating, water
- Interest on loans for home improvements
- Home repairs and maintenance
Deduction limits:
- Maximum deduction: €1,500 per year (approximately)
- Calculated based on room area as percentage of total home area
- Must maintain detailed records and proof of usage
2. OFFICE EQUIPMENT AND SUPPLIES (Työvälineet)
===============================================
Small tools and equipment used exclusively for work can be deducted
if they cost less than €850 per item.
Eligible items:
- Office furniture: desks, chairs, shelves
- Computer equipment: keyboards, monitors, mice
- Office supplies: paper, pens, folders
- Specialized work tools
Requirements:
- Items must be used exclusively for business purposes
- Small items (<€850) are deducted in full in purchase year
- Larger items may be depreciated over useful life
- Must keep receipts and documentation
3. PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AND TRAINING (Koulutus)
====================================================
Costs for professional development directly related to your business
are generally deductible.
Deductible training:
- Professional courses and certifications
- Seminars and conferences
- Online training platforms
- Books and educational materials for professional purposes
- Trade publications
Requirements:
- Training must relate to your current trade or profession
- Cannot be initial training before starting business
- Must maintain certificates and completion records
- Travel expenses to training are also deductible
4. VEHICLE AND FUEL EXPENSES (Polttoaine ja ajoneuvokulukulut)
=============================================================
If a vehicle is used for business purposes, related costs may be deductible.
Deductible expenses:
- Fuel and energy for business trips
- Vehicle maintenance and repairs
- Vehicle insurance (portion for business use)
- Depreciation on business vehicle
- Vehicle registration and license fees
Recordkeeping:
- Must maintain a mileage log for business vs. personal use
- Keep receipts for fuel and maintenance
- Record business purpose of each trip
- Percentage of business use must be documented
Special rules:
- Commuting to regular workplace is NOT deductible
- Only business-related trips qualify
- Delivery and client visit trips are deductible
5. MEALS AND HOSPITALITY (Ruoka ja vieraanvaraisuus)
====================================================
Business meals and entertainment expenses may be partially deductible.
Deductible meals:
- Meals with clients and business partners
- Meals during business travel
- Working meals (if necessary for business)
Deduction rate:
- 50% of meal expenses are typically deductible
- Entertainment expenses follow similar rules
- Must document business purpose and attendees
Non-deductible:
- Personal meals
- Staff meals at company offices (limited deduction)
- Alcohol may have restrictions
6. TRAVEL EXPENSES (Matkakulut)
==============================
Business travel expenses are deductible.
Deductible costs:
- Accommodation during business travel
- Transportation (flights, trains, car rentals)
- Meals during travel (partially)
- Taxi fares for business purposes
Non-deductible:
- Commuting to regular workplace
- Personal holiday expenses
- Commuting between home and office (if not traveling further)
7. INSURANCE AND PROFESSIONAL MEMBERSHIP (Vakuutukset ja jäsenmaksut)
=====================================================================
Professional and business insurance is deductible.
Deductible:
- Professional liability insurance
- Equipment and property insurance for business use
- Professional association membership fees
- Trade union fees
Documentation:
- Policy documents
- Receipts and invoices
- Proof of business-related need
8. DEPRECIATION (Poistot)
============================
Large equipment and assets are depreciated over their useful life.
Depreciation rules:
- Straight-line depreciation is most common
- Different assets have different useful lives
- Office equipment: typically 3-10 years
- Vehicles: typically 5-8 years
- Computer equipment: typically 3 years
Requirements:
- Asset cost must exceed €850
- Must be documented with purchase receipts
- Annual depreciation is deductible
9. HOME OFFICE AREA CALCULATION EXAMPLE
========================================
If your home is 100 m² and you dedicate 20 m² to exclusive office use:
- Office percentage: 20/100 = 20%
- If annual housing costs are €10,000
- Deductible home office portion: €10,000 × 20% = €2,000
However, ceiling limits apply and must be checked against current regulations.
10. IMPORTANT DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
=========================================
For all deductions, maintain:
- Original receipts and invoices
- Proof of payment (bank statements, credit card bills)
- Business purpose documentation
- Description of how items are used
- Records for at least 6 years (accounting requirement)
Non-deductible expenses in general:
- Personal living expenses
- Commuting costs to regular workplace
- Private insurance (health, life insurance for personal use)
- Entertainment for personal guests
Note: These rules are for informational purposes. Specific deductions
depend on individual circumstances, business type, and current tax
legislation. Consult a professional accountant or tax advisor for
personalized advice.
